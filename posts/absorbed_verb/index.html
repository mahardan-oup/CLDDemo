absorbed

verb əbˈzɔːbd
absorbed is the past participle of absorb

  1. to absorb something like liquid is to soak it up
  2. to be absorbed in something is to be interested in it and give it all your attention
  3. to soak up a liquid or gas
  4. To absorb a shock or impact is to reduce its effects.
  5. to take up a person’s attention or time

Etymology: from Latin absorbere ‘to suck in’

Root word: absorb (verb)

Related roots: None

Related words: absorbs (verb), absorbing (verb), absorption (noun)
