abided

verb əˈbaɪdɪd
abided is the past participle of abide

  1. you cannot abide something or someone when you don’t like them at all
  2. to abide by a rule or promise is to keep to it
  3. If you cannot abide something, you detest it or cannot bear it.

Etymology: from Old English abidan ‘to wait’

Root word: abide (verb)

Related roots: None

Related words: abides (verb), abiding (verb), abode (verb)
