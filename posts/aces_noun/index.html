aces

noun ˈeɪsɪz
aces is the plural form of ace

  1. a playing card with an A in the corner and a large emblem of the suit in the centre
  2. a very clever or skilful person
  3. in tennis, an ace is a serve that the other player cannot return
  4. a playing card with one spot
  5. (in tennis) a serve that is too fast or well aimed for the other player to return

Etymology: from Latin as ‘unit’

Root word: ace (noun)

Related roots: None
