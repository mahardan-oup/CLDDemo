abolishes

verb əˈbɒlɪʃɪz
abolishes is the third person present form of abolish

  1. to abolish a rule or custom is to get rid of it
  2. to end a law, system, or custom

Etymology: from Latin abolere ‘to destroy’

Root word: abolish (verb)

Related roots: None

Related words: abolishing (verb), abolished (verb)
