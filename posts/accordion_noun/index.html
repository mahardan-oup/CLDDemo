accordion

noun əˈkɔːdiən

  1. a portable musical instrument which you play by squeezing the sides and pressing on the keys
  2. a portable musical instrument with bellows and a set of piano-type keys at one end, played by squeezing and stretching it and pressing the keys

Etymology: from Italian accordare ‘to tune an instrument’

Root word: accordion (noun) is a root form in the accordion word family.

Related roots: None

Related words: accordions (noun)
