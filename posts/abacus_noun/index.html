abacus

noun ˈæbəkəs

  1. a frame with rows of sliding beads, used for counting and doing sums

Etymology: from Greek abax, a name for a board covered with grains of sand, used for counting. The Greek word came from a Hebrew word meaning ‘dust’.

Root word: abacus (noun) is a root form in the abacus word family.

Related roots: None
