accent

noun ak-sent ækˈsent OR ˈæksent OR ˈæksənt

  1. an accent is the way that people from a particular area pronounce words
  2. the accent in a word is the part you speak more strongly than the rest of it
  3. an accent is a special mark put over a letter in a foreign word, to show its pronunciation
  4. the way a person pronounces the words of a language
  5. the emphasis or stress used in pronouncing a word
  6. a mark placed over a letter to show how it is pronounced, e.g. in résumé

Etymology: from Latin cantus ‘song’

Root word: accent (noun) is a root form in the accent word family.

Related roots: accent (verb)

Related words: accents (noun)
